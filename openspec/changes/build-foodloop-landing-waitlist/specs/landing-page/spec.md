## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Georgian-first public landing page
The system SHALL provide a responsive public landing page for FoodLoop that presents the product in Georgian-first copy, keeps the FoodLoop brand name prominent, and renders Georgian script without clipping, overflow, or degraded readability.

#### Scenario: Visitor opens the homepage on desktop
- **WHEN** a visitor opens the homepage on a desktop viewport
- **THEN** the page displays a polished FoodLoop landing experience with Georgian-first hero copy, clear brand presence, and no text overflow.

#### Scenario: Visitor opens the homepage on mobile
- **WHEN** a visitor opens the homepage on a mobile viewport
- **THEN** the page adapts to the viewport with readable Georgian text, accessible controls, and no overlapping sections.

### Requirement: Coming-soon marketplace positioning
The system SHALL present FoodLoop as a coming-soon Tbilisi marketplace for discounted surplus food without claiming that live map search, live offer inventory, reservation, payment, pickup tracking, or app downloads are currently available.

#### Scenario: Visitor reviews product messaging
- **WHEN** a visitor reads the landing page messaging and calls to action
- **THEN** the page invites the visitor to join the waitlist and does not provide live reserve, download, map search, or payment actions.

### Requirement: Product-led landing page sections
The system SHALL include sections for the hero, waitlist call to action, how FoodLoop works, coming offer examples, impact snapshot, partner invitation, and final waitlist call to action.

#### Scenario: Visitor scans the page
- **WHEN** a visitor scrolls through the homepage
- **THEN** the page communicates what FoodLoop is, how the future service will work, what kinds of surplus food may appear, why it matters, and how users or partners can register interest.

### Requirement: Honest trust and asset usage
The system SHALL reuse suitable existing FoodLoop/Tilda copy and imagery while excluding placeholder testimonials, placeholder contact details, unconfirmed partner logos, and fake credibility claims.

#### Scenario: Page renders trust content
- **WHEN** the landing page displays proof, partner, or community content
- **THEN** it uses honest category-based or mission-based language and does not display unconfirmed real partner logos or fabricated testimonials.
